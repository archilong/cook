# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cook Picture is for families, couples, roommates, and close friends who coordinate everyday cooking together. Users open it in casual home contexts, often on a phone, to record private recipes, share dishes with a household, request meals, assign cooking tasks, and keep reminders lightweight.

## Product Purpose

Cook Picture exists to make shared home cooking feel warm, personal, and organized. Success means users can quickly find what to cook, share a family recipe space, request or accept cooking tasks, and recognize the app as their own private kitchen through customizable visuals.

## Brand Personality

Warm, fresh, playful. The product should feel like a private kitchen notebook with a gentle companion quality: approachable, tidy, and emotionally soft without becoming childish or cluttered.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic enterprise dashboards, stark black-and-white utility apps, heavy SaaS card grids, sterile admin panels, and overly dense data-table layouts. Also avoid copying a specific mini-program screen literally; use the reference direction of soft mint freshness, rounded cards, friendly food imagery, and customizable personal space while keeping Cook Picture's own structure.

## Design Principles

1. Make the kitchen feel personal: support user-selected background, cover, avatar, icon, and empty-state imagery where they help the space feel owned.
2. Keep action paths obvious: recipe browsing, family sharing, ordering, reminders, and settings must remain faster than the decoration around them.
3. Use warmth as structure: soft surfaces, friendly spacing, and gentle illustrations should clarify hierarchy rather than hide controls.
4. Design for shared home rituals: emphasize family space, meal requests, upcoming tasks, and lightweight updates over abstract productivity metrics.
5. Let customization be recoverable: every custom visual should have a default, preview, replace, and reset path.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Prefer readable contrast, large touch targets, clear text labels beside decorative icons, and reduced reliance on color alone. Decorative custom images must not be required to understand navigation or state. Motion should be subtle and avoid blocking task completion.
